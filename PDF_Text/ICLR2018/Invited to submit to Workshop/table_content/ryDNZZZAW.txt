Following the experimentsin (Ganin et al., 2016), we combine four popular digits datasets-MNIST, MNIST-M, SVHN, andSynthDigits to build the multi-source domain dataset. MNIST is a handwritten digits database with60, 000 training examples, and 10, 000 testing examples. The digits have been size-normalized andcentered in a 28 × 28 image. MNIST-M is generated by blending digits from the original MNISTset over patches randomly extracted from color photos from BSDS500 (Arbelaez et al., 2011; Ganinet al., 2016). It has 59, 001 training images and 9, 001 testing images with 32 × 32 resolution.
